# BMES / Medtronic Application Requirements

Track: Electrical / Computer Science / AI.

Required sections:

- Executive Summary, 500 words max.
- Problem Description, 125 words max.
- Project Objective, 125 words max.
- Final Design Documentation, 250 words max, including standards and risk analysis.
- Prototype Uploaded Files.
- Functional Proof, 250 words max.
- Prior Art / Patentability, 250 words max.
- Anticipated Regulatory Pathway, 125 words max.
- Estimated Manufacturing Costs, 250 words max.
- Potential Market and Impact, 250 words max.
- References / Acknowledgements.

Judging alignment:

- Product need and market potential: BME students and instructors need inspectable bridges from circuit diagrams/prompts to executable models and guided learning.
- Device utility and novelty: MATLAB-native model-grounded tutor, not just a chatbot.
- Technical feasibility: live evidence for three benchmarks and packaged `.mltbx` release.
- Budget/economic plan: software deployment, academic MATLAB infrastructure, API usage, instructor setup.
- Writing clarity: keep all claims linked to evidence and limitations.

Regulatory positioning:

- Current product is educational software / design-assistance software.
- It is not patient-facing and does not diagnose, monitor, treat, or control patient care.
- If extended into patient-connected verification or clinical decision support, regulatory analysis must be revisited.
